Reliability · Safety · Resilience
TANDIB · Group 2 · 16 June 2026

The Concepts I Implemented

My responsibility was to implement three attributes of dependability — reliability, safety and resilience (Sommerville Ch.10) — and to prove them through testing.

How I implemented Reliability

  • I made important actions all-or-nothing: either every step of an action succeeds, or the whole action is undone and nothing is left half-finished.
  • I ensured the system always gives consistent results, so its state never contradicts what the user is told.
  • I applied verification and validation — automated tests that re-check the correct behaviour after every change, so reliability cannot silently break.

How I implemented Safety

  • I made sure the system cannot enter harmful states — actions that would cause loss or impossible values are blocked rather than trusted not to happen.
  • I modelled valid steps so the system refuses any invalid or out-of-order action.

How I implemented Resilience

  • I designed the system to keep doing its core job even when a part it depends on misbehaves.
  • When something goes wrong, the user receives a clear, honest message instead of a crash, so a single failure does not bring the whole service down — it degrades gracefully.

Reflection

The biggest lesson was to design for failure, not just for success — to ask "what happens when this goes wrong?" at every step. I also learned the value of verification and validation: moving from "it seems to work" to "we can prove it works" with automated tests. That shift is, to me, the heart of what software engineering means.
